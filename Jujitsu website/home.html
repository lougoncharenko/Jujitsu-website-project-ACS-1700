What is Jujitsu?

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a martial art sport that takes place on the ground. Unlike other martial arts, Brazilian Jujitsu does not use strikes and/or kicks rather jiu jitsu athletes focus on close-contact “grappling” holds and techniques. The goal of Brazillian Jujitsu is to submit your oppenent in a non-violent nature using chokes and joint-manipulations. Brazillian Jujitsu using the knowledge of human anatomy, leverage, time and pressure to effectively grapple.

Why practice Jujitsu?

Like most sports, Jujitsu offers both physical and mental benefits.

Jujitsu provides many physical benefits because it combines mobility, explosive movements, cardio and strength. Jujitsu helps develops increased strength and cardiovascularity as well as promotes weight loss or maintenance and improves muscle tones. Due to the fact that you’re constantly responding to another person’s movements, jiu jitsu heightens your body awareness and improves your balance and reactive ability.

Jujitsu is an incredible mental workout. The sport challenges your mind by requiring you to learn a new activity and all the movement, techniques and submissions that come along with it. Jujitsu has often been compared to a chess game, in the sense that you are always thinking ahead and creating strategies based on your opponent's movements. Jujitsu trains you to think quickly while remaining calm and controlled even when you are put in a defensive situation.
